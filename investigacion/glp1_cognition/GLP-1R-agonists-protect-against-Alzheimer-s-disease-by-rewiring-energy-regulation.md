Daxiang Na & Marc Schneeberger Pané
A study from Zhang, Chen, Feng and colleagues 
investigates the multifaceted role of GLP-1R 
agonists in different brain cell types. They show 
that GLP-1R agonists can reduce amyloidosis 
and mitigate Alzheimer’s disease symptoms in 
mice by activating AMPK signaling, regulating 
neuronal amyloid-β metabolism and 
promoting microglial phagocytosis.

in aging and public health. Over 55 million people worldwide live with 
dementia, and AD accounts for the majority of cases. In the USA alone, 
care for individuals with dementia cost an estimated US $355 billion 
in 2021 (ref. 1), excluding the immense burden of unpaid caregiving 
that is provided by families. More than a century after Alzheimer first 
described the disease, truly effective therapies remain elusive. Despite 
its well-defined pathological hallmarks — such as the accumulation of 
misfolded proteins and chronic neuroinflammation — the origins of 
AD remain complex. Intriguingly, mounting evidence has revealed a 
notable overlap between neurodegenerative and metabolic disorders, 
particularly type 2 diabetes mellitus2. Both diseases share common 
pathophysiological features, including impaired glucose metabolism, 
insulin resistance, chronic inflammation and disrupted cellular energy 
homeostasis. Metabolic dysfunction in diabetes can exacerbate neu-

cycle. This convergence has opened the door to a new line of thinking: a
