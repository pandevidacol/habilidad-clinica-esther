Highlights
GLP-1 analogs can cross the blood brain barrier and stimulate the GLP-1 receptor in the brain
Stimulation of the GLP-1 receptor has shown beneficial effects on mitochondrial function, protein 
aggregation, neuroinflammation, synaptic plasticity, learning and memory in multiple experimental 
models of PD and AD.
Parkinson’s disease and Type 2 diabetes share many common pathophysiological mechanisms
A process analogous to peripheral insulin resistance may initiate/exacerbate neurodegeneration in
