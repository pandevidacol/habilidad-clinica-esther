## Abstract / Summary
Background: It has recently become apparent that neuroinflammation may play a significant role in
Parkinson's disease (PD). This is also the case in animal paradigms of the disease. The potential
neuroprotective action of the glucagon-like peptide 1 receptor (GLP-1R) agonist exendin-4 (EX-4), which
is protective against cytokine mediated apoptosis and may stimulate neurogenesis, was investigated In
paradigms of PD.
