## Abstract / Summary
, this study revealed that Salmonella can activate the 
host’s CNS via the vagus nerve, resulting in anxiety-like behavior and 
localized CNS inflammation. Simultaneously, vagus nerve blocking 
inhibited the disruption of gut microbiota and its metabolites induced by 
Salmonella infection and colonic damage. Furthermore, it elucidated the 
role of oral GABA intervention in conferring resistance to Salmonella 
infection. This study underscores the critical role of the vagus nerve in 
relaying signals of gut infection to the brain and posits that targeting the 
gut-brain axis may offer a novel and efficacious approach to preventing 
gastrointestinal infections and associated behavioral abnormalities.
