## Abstract / Summary
The microbiome-gut-brain axis paradigm explains that alterations in the central nervous 
system and behavior may be secondary to functional changes in the gut in general and 
more specifically the enteric nervous system. An unfavorable development of the 
intestinal microbial ecosystem, leading to e.g. a diminished microbial diversity, may 
play a central role. This paper outlines, and describes the theoretical basis of, a novel 
integrative model explaining the etiology and pathogenesis of ADHD in a microbiota-
gut-brain context, taking into account the complexity of the bi-directional signaling 
between the gut and the brain.
